Map\n\nThe **Downtown Line** (**DTL**) is a [medium-capacity](/wiki/Medium-capacity_rail_system \"Medium-capacity rail system\") [Mass Rapid Transit](/wiki/Mass_Rapid_Transit_\\(Singapore\\) \"Mass Rapid Transit (Singapore)\") (MRT) line in [Singapore](/wiki/Singapore \"Singapore\"). It runs from [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang_MRT/LRT_station \"Bukit Panjang MRT/LRT station\") station in the north-west of the country towards [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\") station in the east via a [loop](/wiki/Circle_route \"Circle route\") around the [city-centre](/wiki/Central_Area,_Singapore \"Central Area, Singapore\"). Coloured blue on the rail map, the line serves 35 stations, all of which are underground.[\\[2\\]](#cite_note-sbstransit.com.sg-2) The DTL is the fifth MRT line on the network to be opened and the third line to be entirely underground. It is the second MRT line to be operated by [ComfortDelGro](/wiki/ComfortDelGro \"ComfortDelGro\")'s [SBS Transit](/wiki/SBS_Transit \"SBS Transit\") after the [North East Line](/wiki/North_East_Line \"North East Line\"). It also serves as an alternative to the [East–West Line](/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"East–West Line (Singapore)\") where it runs roughly geographically parallel from [Bugis](/wiki/Bugis_MRT_station \"Bugis MRT station\") to [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\") station.\n\nOriginally planned as three separate lines, the lines merged into the Downtown Line in 2007 and construction began in three stages. Stage 1, from [Bugis](/wiki/Bugis_MRT_station \"Bugis MRT station\") to [Chinatown](/wiki/Chinatown_MRT_station \"Chinatown MRT station\") stations[\\[a\\]](#cite_note-5) opened in 2013, followed by Stage 2 from [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang_MRT/LRT_station \"Bukit Panjang MRT/LRT station\") to [Rochor](/wiki/Rochor_MRT_station \"Rochor MRT station\") stations[\\[b\\]](#cite_note-6) opening in 2015. The third and final stage, from [Fort Canning](/wiki/Fort_Canning_MRT_station \"Fort Canning MRT station\") to [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\") stations,[\\[c\\]](#cite_note-7) opened in 2017. At 41.9 kilometres (26.0 mi), the DTL is the longest underground and [automated](/wiki/Automatic_train_operation \"Automatic train operation\") [rapid transit](/wiki/Rapid_transit \"Rapid transit\") line in Singapore.[\\[1\\]](#cite_note-DTL-Stages-1) It utilises the [Bombardier Movia C951](/wiki/Bombardier_Movia_C951/C951A \"Bombardier Movia C951/C951A\") [electric multiple unit](/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit \"Electric multiple unit\") (EMU) and runs in a three-car formation, and is Singapore's second medium-capacity rail line after the [Circle Line](/wiki/Circle_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"Circle Line (Singapore)\").\n\nThe line is set to have new stations and extensions in the 2020s and 2030s. An [infill station](/wiki/Infill_station \"Infill station\") initially constructed as part of Stage 2, [Hume](/wiki/Hume_MRT_station \"Hume MRT station\"), began operations in February 2025.[\\[5\\]](#cite_note-:0-8) Stage 3e, a two-station extension consisting of [Xilin](/wiki/Xilin_MRT_station \"Xilin MRT station\") and [Sungei Bedok](/wiki/Sungei_Bedok_MRT_station \"Sungei Bedok MRT station\"), is under construction and is scheduled to begin operations from the second half of 2026.[\\[6\\]](#cite_note-9) By 2H 2026, it will be about 45 kilometres (28 mi) long with 37 stations, and will serve more than half a million commuters daily.[\\[7\\]](#cite_note-LTA_Downtown_Features-10) A two-station extension to connect with the [North–South Line](/wiki/North%E2%80%93South_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"North–South Line (Singapore)\") at [Sungei Kadut](/wiki/Sungei_Kadut_MRT_station \"Sungei Kadut MRT station\") is also being planned and set to open in 2035.[\\[8\\]](#cite_note-:1-11)\n\nHistory\n-------\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=1 \"Edit section: History\")\\]\n\n### Background and inception\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=2 \"Edit section: Background and inception\")\\]\n\nAt the official opening of the [Dover station](/wiki/Dover_MRT_station \"Dover MRT station\") on 23 October 2001, then-Minister for Communications and Information Technology [Yeo Cheow Tong](/wiki/Yeo_Cheow_Tong \"Yeo Cheow Tong\") announced three MRT lines, two of which―the Bukit Timah Line and the northern part of the Eastern Region Line―are part of today's DTL. The Bukit Timah Line aimed to alleviate the traffic problems along the Bukit Timah and Dunearn Road corridor, and provide a direct link to the city area for residents in [Choa Chu Kang](/wiki/Choa_Chu_Kang \"Choa Chu Kang\") and [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang \"Bukit Panjang\"). The proposed Eastern Region Line, slated to be a loop in the eastern regions, aimed to relieve the passenger load on the often-congested [East–West Line](/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"East–West Line (Singapore)\") and benefiting those living in [Tampines](/wiki/Tampines \"Tampines\"), [Bedok](/wiki/Bedok \"Bedok\") and [Marine Parade](/wiki/Marine_Parade \"Marine Parade\").[\\[9\\]](#cite_note-The_DTL_book-12): 16―17 [\\[10\\]](#cite_note-13)[\\[11\\]](#cite_note-stcirclelineart-14)\n\nOn 14 June 2005, the [Land Transport Authority](/wiki/Land_Transport_Authority \"Land Transport Authority\") (LTA) announced the Downtown Extension (renamed to \"Downtown Line Stage 1\"), which would serve the New Downtown at [Marina Bay](/wiki/Marina_Bay,_Singapore \"Marina Bay, Singapore\") area, where an [integrated resort](/wiki/Integrated_resort \"Integrated resort\") ([Marina Bay Sands](/wiki/Marina_Bay_Sands \"Marina Bay Sands\")) and Singapore's second botanical garden ([Gardens by the Bay](/wiki/Gardens_by_the_Bay \"Gardens by the Bay\")) were to be located. The 3.4-kilometre (2.1-mile) extension, estimated to cost S$1.4 billion, was initially announced to be part of the Circle Line.[\\[12\\]](#cite_note-15) On 27 April 2007, then Transport Minister [Raymond Lim](/wiki/Raymond_Lim \"Raymond Lim\") announced that the Downtown Line would be built in three stages stretching 40 kilometres (25 miles) with 33 stations.[\\[13\\]](#cite_note-16)[\\[14\\]](#cite_note-17)[\\[15\\]](#cite_note-18)[\\[16\\]](#cite_note-19)\n\n### Construction and opening\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=3 \"Edit section: Construction and opening\")\\]\n\n[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Construction_of_the_Downtown_Line_opposite_Clarke_Quay%2C_Singapore_-_20121006-01.jpg/250px-Construction_of_the_Downtown_Line_opposite_Clarke_Quay%2C_Singapore_-_20121006-01.jpg)](/wiki/File:Construction_of_the_Downtown_Line_opposite_Clarke_Quay,_Singapore_-_20121006-01.jpg)\n\nThe construction of DTL tunnels near Clarke Quay. The diversion of the [Singapore River](/wiki/Singapore_River \"Singapore River\") for construction works for the tunnels between the [Chinatown](/wiki/Chinatown_MRT_station \"Chinatown MRT station\") and [Fort Canning](/wiki/Fort_Canning_MRT_station \"Fort Canning MRT station\") stations was considered an engineering feat at the time.[\\[17\\]](#cite_note-20)[\\[18\\]](#cite_note-21)[\\[19\\]](#cite_note-22)\n\n[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/DT32_Tampines_Exit_D.jpg/250px-DT32_Tampines_Exit_D.jpg)](/wiki/File:DT32_Tampines_Exit_D.jpg)\n\nA separate entrance for the DTL platforms for Tampines MRT station, which is not directly connected to the EWL platforms of the station.\n\nThe line was built in 3 stages: Stage 1 of the Downtown Line, stretching 4.3-kilometre (2.7 mi), started construction in January 2008 at [Chinatown station](/wiki/Chinatown_MRT_station \"Chinatown MRT station\"),[\\[9\\]](#cite_note-The_DTL_book-12): 30  where platform provisions were built to facilitate an interchange station during the construction of the [North East Line](/wiki/North_East_Line \"North East Line\"). It began service on 22 December 2013,[\\[20\\]](#cite_note-23) with its official inauguration made on the day before by Prime Minister [Lee Hsien Loong](/wiki/Lee_Hsien_Loong \"Lee Hsien Loong\").[\\[21\\]](#cite_note-24)\n\nStage 2, first conceptualized and announced as the Bukit Timah line on 23 October 2001, is 16.6 kilometres (10.3 mi) long with 12 stations connecting [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang_MRT/LRT_station \"Bukit Panjang MRT/LRT station\") and [Rochor](/wiki/Rochor_MRT_station \"Rochor MRT station\") stations, including four interchange stations. Construction for Stage 2 began on 3 July 2009 with a groundbreaking ceremony at [Beauty World station](/wiki/Beauty_World_MRT_station \"Beauty World MRT station\").[\\[22\\]](#cite_note-25)[\\[23\\]](#cite_note-26) The line inaugurated on 27 December 2015, with free travel for all 18 stations from 27 December 2015 to 1 January 2016.[\\[24\\]](#cite_note-27)[\\[25\\]](#cite_note-Stage_2-28)[\\[26\\]](#cite_note-29)\n\nStage 3 goes from [Chinatown](/wiki/Chinatown_MRT_station \"Chinatown MRT station\") to [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\"). The station locations and finalised route were unveiled on 20 August 2010.[\\[27\\]](#cite_note-30)[\\[28\\]](#cite_note-31) Stage 3 of the Downtown Line is 21 kilometres (13 mi) long and serve 16 stations.[\\[1\\]](#cite_note-DTL-Stages-1)[\\[29\\]](#cite_note-32) The route opened on 21 October 2017,[\\[30\\]](#cite_note-33) with free travel for all DTL stations on the first two days of the line completion.[\\[31\\]](#cite_note-34)[\\[32\\]](#cite_note-35)\n\nThe line, with an estimated cost of S$12 billion, was considered the government's most ambitious rail project. The cost exceeded those of the Circle Line (at $6.7 billion) and the North East Line (at $4.6 billion). Final costs of building the line may reach an estimated about S$20.7 billion, up more than 70% from the initial estimation, due to sharp rises in construction costs and a number of changes in plans.[\\[33\\]](#cite_note-36)[\\[9\\]](#cite_note-The_DTL_book-12): 19 \n\nIn October 2014, it was announced that Stage 2's opening would be pushed back to the first quarter of 2016. This was because one of the main contractors for the DTL2, Alpine Bau (which was building [King Albert Park](/wiki/King_Albert_Park_MRT_station \"King Albert Park MRT station\"), [Sixth Avenue](/wiki/Sixth_Avenue_MRT_station \"Sixth Avenue MRT station\") and [Tan Kah Kee](/wiki/Tan_Kah_Kee_MRT_station \"Tan Kah Kee MRT station\")), went bankrupt in mid-2013.[\\[34\\]](#cite_note-37) On 28 June 2015, [Transport Minister](/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_\\(Singapore\\) \"Ministry of Transport (Singapore)\") [Lui Tuck Yew](/wiki/Lui_Tuck_Yew \"Lui Tuck Yew\") announced that the delay was \"completely resolved\" by the authorities and Stage 2's opening date was brought forward to 27 December 2015.[\\[35\\]](#cite_note-38)[\\[36\\]](#cite_note-39) The DTL 3 started operations on 21 October 2017, officially opened by Transport Minister [Khaw Boon Wan](/wiki/Khaw_Boon_Wan \"Khaw Boon Wan\").[\\[37\\]](#cite_note-40)[\\[38\\]](#cite_note-41)[\\[39\\]](#cite_note-42)\n\nTo serve the line, the [Gali Batu Depot](/wiki/Gali_Batu_Depot \"Gali Batu Depot\") was opened in December 2015 to stable 42 trains. It is situated at part of the former Kwong Hou Sua Teochew Cemetery off Woodlands Road.[\\[40\\]](#cite_note-channelnewsasia.com-43) Also in February that year, the Land Transport Authority announced that the capacity of the depot will be expanded to stable 81 trains by 2019.[\\[41\\]](#cite_note-44)\n\nOn 7 March 2019, [Hume station](/wiki/Hume_MRT_station \"Hume MRT station\") was announced to be opened by the second quarter of 2025 to better serve Hillview residents.[\\[5\\]](#cite_note-:0-8)[\\[42\\]](#cite_note-Tan_2020-45) The station was opened on 28 February 2025, ahead of the original timeline.\n\n### Future\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=4 \"Edit section: Future\")\\]\n\nOn 17 January 2013, plans were announced for a southern extension extending from [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\") to connect with the [Eastern Region Line](/wiki/Eastern_Region_Line \"Eastern Region Line\") (ERL); the ERL has since been subsumed into the Thomson–East Coast Line. The extension was to provide more travel options and enhance connectivity for the residents along the East Coast.[\\[43\\]](#cite_note-46)[\\[44\\]](#cite_note-47) The Downtown Line 3 extension (DTL3e) was finalised and announced on 15 August 2014, in conjunction with the announcement of the Thomson–East Coast Line.[\\[45\\]](#cite_note-TEL-48) Two stations, [Xilin MRT station](/wiki/Xilin_MRT_station \"Xilin MRT station\") and [Sungei Bedok MRT station](/wiki/Sungei_Bedok_MRT_station \"Sungei Bedok MRT station\"), will be added to the Downtown Line, with Sungei Bedok as an interchange station with the Thomson–East Coast Line. It will lengthen the line by 2.2 km (1.4 mi).[\\[45\\]](#cite_note-TEL-48) Expected to be completed in the second half of 2026, Stage 3e will connect the current [East–West Line](/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"East–West Line (Singapore)\") at Expo station and the future TEL at [Sungei Bedok station](/wiki/Sungei_Bedok_MRT_station \"Sungei Bedok MRT station\").[\\[46\\]](#cite_note-49)\n\nAn additional extension to the Downtown Line to [Sungei Kadut](/wiki/Sungei_Kadut_MRT_station \"Sungei Kadut MRT station\") station from [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang_MRT/LRT_station \"Bukit Panjang MRT/LRT station\") station was announced on 25 May 2019 by the LTA. The extension is set to cut down travel time to the downtown area by up to 30 minutes for those living in the northwestern area of Singapore. The extension is expected to be completed by the mid-2030s. A study will also be conducted to determine the stops to be added between the Sungei Kadut and the existing Bukit Panjang stations.[\\[8\\]](#cite_note-:1-11)\n\nOn 6 December 2024, it was announced that Stage 3e will commence operation in the second half of 2026.[\\[47\\]](#cite_note-50)\n\nOn 6 January 2025, a 4 km (2.5 mi) extension to the future Sungei Kadut station was announced, including an additional unnamed station between Sungei Kadut station and Bukit Panjang station, slated to begin operation in 2035.[\\[48\\]](#cite_note-51)[\\[49\\]](#cite_note-52)\n\n### Notable incidents\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=5 \"Edit section: Notable incidents\")\\]\n\nA fire occurred on a maintenance locomotive which was carrying out works at [Mattar](/wiki/Mattar_MRT_station \"Mattar MRT station\") station on 24 May 2024 at around 1:00 am. This resulted in the section between [Fort Canning](/wiki/Fort_Canning_MRT_station \"Fort Canning MRT station\") and Mattar stations to be closed as the locomotive could not be moved until heat from its body dissipated. The fire was put out by [SCDF](/wiki/Singapore_Civil_Defence_Force \"Singapore Civil Defence Force\") and one staff member was conveyed to the hospital for smoke inhalation. Train services at the affected MRT stations resumed at 7:26 am after more than an hour since the start of train service.[\\[50\\]](#cite_note-53)\n\nNetwork and operations\n----------------------\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=6 \"Edit section: Network and operations\")\\]\n\n### Route\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=7 \"Edit section: Route\")\\]\n\n[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/MRT_Route_Map_DT.svg/330px-MRT_Route_Map_DT.svg.png)](/wiki/File:MRT_Route_Map_DT.svg)\n\nGeographically accurate map of the Downtown Line.[\\[51\\]](#cite_note-54)\n\nThe Downtown Line generally goes in an east–west direction from [Bukit Panjang](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang \"Bukit Panjang\") in the [northwestern side](/wiki/West_Region,_Singapore \"West Region, Singapore\") of Singapore, through the [downtown area of Singapore](/wiki/Central_Area,_Singapore \"Central Area, Singapore\"), to [Tampines](/wiki/Tampines \"Tampines\") and the [Singapore Expo](/wiki/Singapore_Expo \"Singapore Expo\") in the [east](/wiki/East_Region,_Singapore \"East Region, Singapore\"). It is 41.9 kilometres (26.0 mi) long and is entirely underground. Before [Bukit Panjang station](/wiki/Bukit_Panjang_MRT/LRT_station \"Bukit Panjang MRT/LRT station\"), there exists a brief northwards extension to the line's depot [Gali Batu Depot](/wiki/Gali_Batu_Depot \"Gali Batu Depot\"). The line begins at the Bukit Panjang station and then goes in the southeast direction, running parallel to Upper Bukit Timah Road and along the Bukit Timah Canal through [Bukit Timah](/wiki/Bukit_Timah \"Bukit Timah\"). The line then enters the [central area](/wiki/Central_Area,_Singapore \"Central Area, Singapore\"), passing through [Little India](/wiki/Little_India_MRT_station \"Little India MRT station\") station before reaching the [Central Business District](/wiki/Central_Business_District \"Central Business District\") via [Bugis](/wiki/Bugis_MRT_station \"Bugis MRT station\") station. The Downtown Line runs parallel to the [Circle Line](/wiki/Circle_Line_\\(Singapore\\) \"Circle Line (Singapore)\") at the [Promenade](/wiki/Promenade_MRT_station \"Promenade MRT station\") and [Bayfront](/wiki/Bayfront_MRT_station \"Bayfront MRT station\") stations. Passing through the [Downtown Core](/wiki/Downtown_Core \"Downtown Core\"), the line interchanges with the [North East Line](/wiki/North_East_Line \"North East Line\") at [Chinatown](/wiki/Chinatown_MRT_station \"Chinatown MRT station\") station.\n\nAfter Chinatown station, the line goes in a northeast direction and under the Singapore River. Between the [Bencoolen](/wiki/Bencoolen_MRT_station \"Bencoolen MRT station\") and [Jalan Besar](/wiki/Jalan_Besar_MRT_station \"Jalan Besar MRT station\") stations, the line intersects itself, the first MRT line to do so. After [Geylang Bahru station](/wiki/Geylang_Bahru_MRT_station \"Geylang Bahru MRT station\"), the line continues in a general eastwards direction, through the industrial areas of [Kampong Ubi](/wiki/Kampong_Ubi \"Kampong Ubi\") and [Kaki Bukit](/wiki/Kaki_Bukit \"Kaki Bukit\") (briefly paralleling Ubi Avenue 2 and Kaki Bukit Avenue 1), before continuing towards the [Tampines Regional Centre](/wiki/Tampines \"Tampines\") and goes southwards, ending at [Changi Business Park](/wiki/Changi_Business_Park \"Changi Business Park\"). The DTL3 extension (DTL3e) from [Expo](/wiki/Expo_MRT_station \"Expo MRT station\") station will extend the line further towards the [Thomson–East Coast Line](/wiki/Thomson%E2%80%93East_Coast_Line \"Thomson–East Coast Line\") at [Sungei Bedok](/wiki/Sungei_Bedok_MRT_station \"Sungei Bedok MRT station\") station.[\\[52\\]](#cite_note-55)\n\n### Stations\n\n\\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Downtown_Line&action=edit&section=8 \"Edit section: Stations\")\\]\n\nStation codes for the line are blue, corresponding to the line's colour on the system map.
